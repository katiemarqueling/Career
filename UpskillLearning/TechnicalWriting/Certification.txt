-Key facts about readers and reader profiles 
    -What a reader profile is
        - A reader profile is a n analysis of your reader's specific needs, backgrounds, abilities, and expereinces. Developing a profile of your readers will 
            give you helpful insight into how they will react to your ideas and how thet will use your document. 
    -The names of the four types of readers
        -Primary (action takes: need to make a decison based on the information you are giving them)
        -Secondary (advisors: experts that infulence decisions)
        -Tertiary (evaluators: will make outside judgements about works; news, companies competitors)
        -Gatekeepers (supervisors: review and approve before sent to primary)

-Key facts about writing plain sentences:
    -The eight guidelines for writing plain sentences
        -make the subject of the sentence what the sentence is about 
        -use the "doer" as the subject of the sentence
        -use a verb to express the action, or what the doer is doing 
        -put the subject of the sentence early in the sentence-eliminate nominalizations
        -eliminate excessive prepositional phrases
        -eliminate redundancy in sentences
        -write sentences that are "breathing length"
    -The four types of sentences used in paragraphs
        -transitional sentence
        -topic sentence
        -support sentence
        -point sentence
